\begin{lemma}
$\langle \tau_{0}, \ldots \rangle \in \Traj(M) \implies \langle \gamma(\tau_{0}), \ldots \rangle \in \Traj(N)$
\end{lemma}

Given a $\tau \in \Traj(M)$ and $\rho \in \Runs(\gamma(A))$, such that $| \tau | = | \rho |$ and $\tau_{n} \in \gamma(\Antecedent(\rho_{n}))$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N} : n < | \tau |$, we must show that $\tau_{n} \in \gamma(\Consequent(\rho_{n}))$. By the refinement assumption, there must exist a $\upsilon \in \Traj(N)$ such that $| \tau | = | \upsilon | = | \rho |$ and $\Vis(\tau) \ll \Vis(\upsilon)$, so $\Vis(\tau_{n}) \subseteq \gamma(\Vis(\upsilon_{n}))$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N} : n < | \tau |$.
